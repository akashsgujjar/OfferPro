Personal Presentation

The most important aspect an interview is how you present yourself and your ideas

The way you present yourself to your employer throughout the entire interview process is critical. Everything from your physical appearance to your demeanor and attitude has a huge impact on the success of your interview. There are steps that you can take before, during, and after the interview in order to leave a positive impression with your employer.

Before your Interview

It is imperative that you get a good night’s sleep in order to be well rested mentally. Interview’s are very stressful events, and require a lot of focus and composure. It is common to feel anxious and overwhelmed. By getting a good night's rest, you give your mind a chance to relax.

Put thought into what you wear. Your outfit is a very important part of your personal presentation. Looking sharp shows that you are a detail oriented person. If you are not sure what to wear, you can always reach out to the human resources office, who will be happy to help you. For detailed descriptions of business formal, refer to the Dress Code. Avoid bright colored clothing when selecting your outfit. Also, make sure that all clothes are pressed or dry-cleaned ahead of the interview.

During your Interview

Begin by standing up and shaking hands with the interviewer. Make eye-contact with them, and smile. Shake hands with a firm grip. Don’t forget to maintain a straight posture.

Show curiosity. Employers love it when you ask questions, as they show your interest in the company and opportunities available.

Do not go out of your way to highlight past accomplishments. Not only are these already stated on your resume, but it can also be viewed as being overconfident or arrogant. Let the employer guide the conversation, and when appropriate, allude to past achievements.

After your Interview

Stand up and shake hands with the employer. Don’t forget to smile. Always thank your employer for the opportunity, as this is a sign of respect for them taking the time to conduct the interview.
