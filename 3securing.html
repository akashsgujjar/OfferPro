5 Steps to Secure a Job Interview

I tell jobseekers in all my workshops that research is key to their job search. I’m being redundant, but it’s true and worth repeating. Whether you’re writing a résumé or cover letter, or preparing for an interview or a networking event, the time you put into research is a tremendous return on investment. This time well spent precedes submitting your résumé and being interviewed for positions advertised or not.

Securing an Interview

Before you learn how to present yourself during an interview, it is important to learn how to arrange an interview first

There are many ways through which people get job interviews, but the majority of people do so through three main methods: Employee Referrals, Employer Career Sites, and Job Fairs.

Employee Referrals

How to get started: List off the people you know who work in the same industry that you are prospecting to enter. Now, reach out to each one of these people via text, email, or any other means. This is a process called 'Networking', a concept where people connect professionally with others. It is proven that employers hire those who have connections with their employees as many managers claim its a great way to recruit the best candidates.


Why use this method: Firstly, in the hiring process itself, your contact could obtain an interview for you with ease. They could also explain key tips about the hiring manager including their interviewing style, potential questions, and many more. More importantly, this method has significantly less competition for the job as other employees have vouched for you to join the company.


Disadvantages: When in the job, there is a high probability that you will be working alongside the employee who referred you. Although this may sound great, it has been shown that a group of workers who know each other have decreased synergy, a weakened end product, and broken bonds among colleagues due to conflicts between the professional and home settings.


Employer Career Sites

How to get started: Make a brief list of possible employers in your field of choice, and visit each of their websites to understand their ideas and values. Then, using the steps shown in the 'Job Search' pages, apply to the respective postings. If they feel you are capable, the company itself will notify you with an interview date and time.


Why use this method: If you do not have any contacts in your industry, but know exactly what company you would like a job in, then this is the best method to obtain an interview. Through this, you can directly establish a connection with the company and show your true capabilities through their interview process.


Disadvantages: Without knowing anyone in the company itself, you will be at a slight disadvantage. You will not have anyone vouching for you from the within, and you will not get any insider tips regarding the hiring manager who will interview you. More importantly, there is a lower chance you will get the interview because of the increased competition.


Job Fairs

How to get started: Use our blog, newspapers, and other sources to collect information regarding when Job Fairs and open-houses for your respective industry or prospective company. Mark the date and time on your calendar and make sure to attend as many of these as possible.


Why use this method: Unlike the other methods, this allows you to directly speak with the hiring managers at these various companies and lets you show who you are as a person. These fairs often allow potential candidates to turn in resumes and schedule job interviews, so it is more productive than waiting for an email response. In addition, this is perfect for networking as you could meet other job seekers, who might know someone who could help you.


Disadvantages: Unfortunately, these career fairs do not allow for meaningful, and thought-given conversations. They are fast-paced, noisy, and there are many people waiting in line to speak with the officials.
