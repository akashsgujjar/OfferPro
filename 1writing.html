What is a Resume?

A resume is a detailed summary of your background, education, prior work experience, and any other applicable skills or knowledge. Often referred to as the most important factor in the application process, a resume is a great way to leave a positive first-impression.

Why start here?

Resumes are a vital first step because regardless of what profession you pursue, your resume will be the first thing you submit. Its contents set the stage for the other parts of the job search process.

Writing A Resume

A piece by piece explanation

The Key Parts of a Successful Resume:

The main contents of a professional resume include your Contact Information, Goal, Education, Work Experience, Applicable Skills, and lastly any other Additional Information. Let's review each one, so you are at the top of the competition.

Contact Information

Make sure that your employers will have all the information they need to contact you. Make sure to include your full name, telephone number with area code, mailing address, and email address.

Goal

State what position you seek in the company (ex: “Seeking part-time accountant job.”) A common mistake that applicants make is by combining different categories.

Education

List your award(s), certification, and/or degree(s) earned throughout your schooling, with the exact dates, and the names of the schools, colleges, or universities that you have acquired them from. Don't forget to list any school activities such as clubs provided further educational benefit.

If you haven’t graduated from your current schooling, be sure to include your current progress (ex: “I have earned 16 out of a total 20 credits towards earning by Bachelors degree).

Work Experience

Include your previous job title, employer name, and at least 5 job requirements from past job. This is important because it highlights your first hand training in the workplace. Do the preceeding steps for each former employer.

Don't worry if you have not had any work experience yet. You can simply substitute this area by showing your experiences in volunteering, sports, school, and other projects that have significant value to your character and skills.

Skills

For this section, just list the tools, devices, programs, licences, and systems that you can use well. Mention other essential skills (such as communication, teamwork, and public speaking) as well as languages you are proficient in.

Additional Information

This is an optional section, but it allows for you to distinguish yourself by mentioning any special talents, or abilities that may interest a future employer.
