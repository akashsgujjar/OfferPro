How to Get Ready for a Job Interview

Do you have a job interview on your schedule? There are a number of steps you can take before (and after) the interview to ensure that you make a terrific impression during the interview. Below are tips on how to prepare for an interview.

Preparing for an Interview

With an interview scheduled, you must now get prepared for it

In order to prepare well for any interview, you must first understand that the three main types of interviews are: Over Phone, In-Person, and Video Call are. After mastering this, you must do extensive research on the employer.

Types of Interviews

Over Phone: As an effort to reduce costs, many companies have switched over to phone interviews. Other times, these phone screenings are used as a preliminary step to decide who recieves an in-person interview

Interview Strategies: Agree upon a date and time for the interview that suits best for you and the employer. Find an appropriate setting in a room without noise and distractions. Know who you will be speaking with, and try to understand their goal. Always prepare meaningful questions in advance that allow you to show what your values are.



In-Person: As the most traditional form of interview, in-person interviews are often considered the best format to judge a candidate.

Interview Strategies: Well before the day of the interview, make sure to ask the employer for the interview agenda, the interviewer's details, any information/materials to bring to the interview, as well as where and when the interview is. Always prepare meaningful questions in advance regardless of whether a question/answer session is explicitly stated in the interview timeline.



Video Call: Also used as an evaluatory method, Video Calls have become the substitute for in-person interviews. Rather than flying a candidate over from distant locations, employers can finally conduct the same interview through a low cost method.

Interview Strategies: Before beginning the interview, it is essential to create the best setting with optimal lighting and a neat background. Also, ensure you know how to operate the application used to conduct the interview to ensure no problems occur. Always give your eye contact by looking at the camera directly and eliminate all distractions. Don't forget to prepare a list of significant questions to ask the interviewer.



What Research should you do?

Before the interview, you must always learn everything about the employer, and the job your are applying for. Explore the employer's website to understand their mission and values. Read over the job description and write out how you qualify for each one. If you already know the interviewing style of the hiring manager, try to form answers to some of the most common interview questions such as:

1) What can you tell me about yourself?

2) Can you list your strengths?

3) What weaknesses do you have?

4) Why should I consider hiring you?


Next, prepare a series of questions that you will ask the interviewer. Make sure these questions don't pertain to vacations and salary, as these will create a bad image. Instead, try to create questions that allow for meaningful conversations. Write these out on a separate sheet of paper and try to memorize them before the interview.
