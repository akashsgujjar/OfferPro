The Job Application

Filling It Out and Writing a Cover Letter

One of the most important parts of the job search process is the application itself. The application often requires you to fill out an application form, write a cover letter, and provide a resume. You already know how to do the latter, so lets focus on the other two key concepts.

Filling it Out

A typical job application has five main parts. It usually consists of your basic personal information, the details of the position you are applying for, education information, employment history, and references.

Personal information: Carefully write out your full name, address, and contact information in the fields provided. Truthfully answer any checkboxes asking about your age, citizenship, and job eligibility requirements.

Employment Description: Fill out this section with honest answers, but be sure not to give an expected wage or salary. If it's too high, you might be presented as unreasonable, but too desperate if low. Just write "standard wage" to be safe

Education Information and Past Employment: Make sure to fill out this section according to the information you have highlighted in your resume. If there is a discrepancy, you might be immediately removed from the list

References: A reference is someone who can assure your future employer about your skills and character. Anyone whom you have spent extensive professional time with such as managers, coworkers, coaches, or volunteer coordinators will suffice. Make sure to have their permission before using their name


The Cover Letter

Unlike the actual application and the resume which present only factual information, the cover letter allows you to explain who you are, what your character is like, and what passions you have. There are four main parts to a successful cover letter: Getting Attention, Showing Interest, Explaining Desire, and Taking Action

Getting Attention: In the first paragraph, get the attention of the hiring manager by giving the reason for applying at their company. Make sure it is not generalized, and suits you.

Create Interest: In the second paragraph, begin to create interest for the hiring manager. Show him/her what distinguishes you from the pack and how it will help them.

Explaining Desire: In the third paragraph, explain why you have a desire for that job in specifics. Use the job requirements to guide you here as these steps are to ensure the hiring manager chooses to meet you in person.

Taking Action: Lastly, call for action by politely asking for an interview
